Sunnyside Scrambler

Created a ride entry with CU Theme Park Engineering and Design club for the 2026 Ride Engineering Competition hosted in Hershey Pennsylvania. The ride is a small scale ride for candy riders that is able to have rotation, vertical displacement of 1ft, and achieve 2G's of acceleration. Additionally, the ride is required to meet ASTM F2291 design standards for amusement ride safety.

Challenges

  • Coming into this project with no prior engineering experience and not having worked on large scale projects required learning to keep up to speed with all changes and modifications to the project. Additionally, becoming an Assistant Project Manager helped me to gain exposure to working with technical documentation and ensuring design safety and ASTM compliance across all subteams.

  • Another challenge encountered was in the transitional phase between ideation and creation, and learning how to adapt to rapid changes in the requirements of our design. For example, early work was put into designing a scissor-lift from scratch, and was later decided to be bought off the market instead. This was changed again further into the project going back to fabricating our own. This process helped me to understand how to be flexible with designs, and not to get attached to early concepts as they will likely change.

Results

  • Created a functional ride. Our ride and documentation package eaned us 2nd Place in the Western Division at the Ride Engineering Competition. Our ride was able to maintain continuous operation for 4.5/6 hours during the competition, which includes downtime due to mandatory disassembly and assembly during the operational period.
  • Co-Authored an 80+ page ASTM F24 Compliance Document and overall documentation package containing a Rider Eligibility Analysis, Patron Containment Analysis, Service Plan, and Risk Assessment.
